The Challenge

Electricity bills keep climbing every cycle while households have no real visibility into which appliances are responsible during peak hours.
Existing smart home apps are good at flipping switches, but they never act on the data they collect or close the loop on consumption.
Voltage surges, current leakage and gas events typically go unnoticed until they have already damaged appliances or harmed residents.
There is no centralised record of what changed in the home, when it changed, or why, which makes diagnosing problems nearly impossible.

The Solution

A predictive optimisation engine studies two weeks of consumption, spots where peak hour energy is being wasted and quietly enforces auto off rules to bring the bill down.
A conversational agent powered by Google Gemini holds natural conversations, performs real actions through tools and remembers facts the resident chooses to share.
A profile band anomaly detector continuously watches voltage and current readings and instantly cuts power the moment something drifts outside the safe envelope.
Every action taken in the house, whether by a person, by an automation, or by the agent itself, is recorded with a reason so the history of the home is always available.

Objectives

Deliver a complete smart home operating layer that gives residents real time control of every connected device in their home.
Reduce peak hour electricity costs through automation rules the system learns from the household’s own consumption history.
Provide a conversational agent that lets the resident interact with the home in plain language, by voice or by chat.
Protect residents from electrical and environmental hazards through continuous anomaly detection and automatic cut off.
Make consumption transparent at room level so a family can see exactly where energy is going and split the monthly bill fairly.
